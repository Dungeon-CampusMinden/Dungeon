# 01 - Learning Alignment and Educational Escape-Room Design

Scope note: This file is research background for later scientific writing and
future versions. Learning-goal mapping and debriefing are not V0 requirements.

## Core Sources

### Biggs 1996 - Constructive Alignment

- Citation: Biggs, J. (1996). Enhancing teaching through constructive alignment.
  Higher Education, 32, 347-364.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.1007/BF00138871>
- Priority: core/background.
- Use: Provides the general didactic basis for aligning learning objectives,
  learning activities, and assessment evidence.
- Wizard relevance: Supports the rule that every riddle should map to a learning
  goal and that every learning goal needs gameplay evidence and reflection.

### Clarke et al. 2017 - EscapED

- Citation: Clarke, S. J., Peel, D. J., Arnab, S., Morini, L., Keegan, H., &
  Wood, O. (2017). EscapED: A Framework for Creating Educational Escape Rooms
  and Interactive Games for Higher/Further Education. International Journal of
  Serious Games, 4(3), 73-86.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.17083/ijsg.v4i3.180>
- Priority: core.
- Use: Direct educational escape-room framework for higher/further education.
- Wizard relevance: Supports the authoring workflow around participants,
  objectives, theme, puzzles, equipment, and evaluation.

### Veldkamp et al. 2020 - Escape Education Systematic Review

- Citation: Veldkamp, A., van de Grint, L., Knippels, M. C. P. J., & van
  Joolingen, W. R. (2020). Escape education: A systematic review on escape rooms
  in education. Educational Research Review, 31, 100364.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.1016/j.edurev.2020.100364>
- Priority: core.
- Use: Systematic review of educational escape-room practice and design aspects.
- Wizard relevance: Strongest source for aligning game mechanics with
  pedagogical goals and for choosing puzzle structures, group size, playtime,
  and technology roles.

### Veldkamp et al. 2021 - Beyond the Early Adopters

- Citation: Veldkamp, A., Knippels, M. C. P. J., & van Joolingen, W. R. (2021).
  Beyond the Early Adopters: Escape Rooms in Science Education. Frontiers in
  Education, 6, 622860.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.3389/feduc.2021.622860>
- Priority: secondary/core.
- Use: Empirical source on science-education escape-room adoption, teacher
  perspectives, implementation barriers, teamwork, and motivation.
- Wizard relevance: Supports treating the wizard as teacher-facing support, not
  just as a level generator.

### Makri et al. 2021 - Digital Educational Escape Rooms

- Citation: Makri, A., Vlachopoulos, D., & Martina, R. A. (2021). Digital Escape
  Rooms as Innovative Pedagogical Tools in Education: A Systematic Literature
  Review. Sustainability, 13(8), 4587.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.3390/su13084587>
- Priority: core.
- Use: Systematic review focused on digital educational escape rooms.
- Wizard relevance: Supports the digital scope of DEER and helps distinguish
  digital affordances from analog escape-room practice.

### Fotaris and Mastoras 2022 - Room2Educ8

- Citation: Fotaris, P., & Mastoras, T. (2022). Room2Educ8: A Framework for
  Creating Educational Escape Rooms Based on Design Thinking Principles.
  Education Sciences, 12(11), 768.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.3390/educsci12110768>
- Priority: core.
- Use: Learner-centered design-thinking framework for educational escape-room
  creation.
- Wizard relevance: Supports wizard steps for learner empathy, objectives,
  constraints, narrative, puzzle design, briefing/debriefing, prototyping, and
  playtesting.

### Guigon et al. 2018 - SEGAM

- Citation: Guigon, G., Humeau, J., & Vermeulen, M. (2018). A Model to Design
  Learning Escape Games: SEGAM. CSEDU 2018, 191-197.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.5220/0006665501910197>
- Priority: core.
- Use: Model for designing learning escape games.
- Wizard relevance: Particularly relevant for riddle graph modeling because it
  links levels, riddles, clues, and educational objectives.

### Botturi and Babazadeh 2020 - Star Model

- Citation: Botturi, L., & Babazadeh, M. (2020). Designing educational escape
  rooms: validating the Star Model. International Journal of Serious Games,
  7(3), 41-57.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.17083/ijsg.v7i3.367>
- Priority: core.
- Use: Validated teacher-facing model for educational escape-room design.
- Wizard relevance: Supports a structured design workflow and the importance of
  debriefing, classroom integration, game flow, and design confidence.

## Secondary and Background Sources

### Sanchez and Plumettaz-Sieber 2019 - Debriefing

- Citation: Sanchez, E., & Plumettaz-Sieber, M. (2019). Teaching and Learning
  with Escape Games From Debriefing to Institutionalization of Knowledge. LNCS
  11385, 242-253.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-030-11548-7_23>
- Priority: core/secondary.
- Use: Treats debriefing as a bridge from gameplay to formal learning.
- Wizard relevance: Supports requiring reflection/debrief prompts in the DEER
  configuration.

### Fanning and Gaba 2007 - Simulation-Based Debriefing

- Citation: Fanning, R. M., & Gaba, D. M. (2007). The Role of Debriefing in
  Simulation-Based Learning. Simulation in Healthcare, 2(2), 115-125.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.1097/SIH.0b013e3180315539>
- Priority: background/secondary.
- Use: Foundational debriefing source for simulation-based learning.
- Wizard relevance: Supports structured post-game reflection even when learning
  is inferred from situated activity rather than only from tests.

### Cheng et al. 2014 - Debriefing Meta-Analysis

- Citation: Cheng, A., Eppich, W., Grant, V., Sherbino, J., Zendejas, B., &
  Cook, D. A. (2014). Debriefing for technology-enhanced simulation: A
  systematic review and meta-analysis. Medical Education, 48(7), 657-666.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.1111/medu.12432>
- Priority: background/secondary.
- Use: Systematic review and meta-analysis on debriefing for
  technology-enhanced simulation.
- Wizard relevance: Provides broader methodological support for using
  debriefing prompts as part of the generated evaluation package.

### Nicholson 2015 - Meaningful Gamification

- Citation: Nicholson, S. (2015). A RECIPE for Meaningful Gamification. In
  Gamification in Education and Business, 1-20.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-319-10208-5_1>
- Priority: background.
- Use: Meaningful gamification framework.
- Wizard relevance: Helps argue against superficial points/badges and toward
  choice, information, play, engagement, reflection, and exposition.

### Nicholson 2018 - Classroom Escape Rooms

- Citation: Nicholson, S. (2018). Creating Engaging Escape Rooms for the
  Classroom. Childhood Education, 94(1), 44-49.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.1080/00094056.2018.1420363>
- Priority: background.
- Use: Practitioner-oriented academic source on classroom escape rooms.
- Wizard relevance: Useful for classroom adaptation and avoiding shallow
  "content as locks" puzzle design.

### Reuter et al. 2020 - Strategies for Creation and Design

- Citation: Reuter, J., Ferreira Dias, M., Amorim, M., Figueiredo, C., &
  Veloso, C. (2020). How to create Educational Escape rooms? Strategies for
  creation and design. TEEM 2020.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.1145/3434780.3436664>
- Priority: secondary.
- Use: Synthesizes creation and design strategies for educational escape rooms.
- Wizard relevance: Useful for related work and for checking whether the wizard
  workflow covers common creation steps.

### ELMET Guide - Practical Educational Escape-Room Guide

- Citation: ELMET Project. Guide for Design, Implementation, Facilitation and
  Evaluation of Educational Escape Rooms.
- URL: <https://www.elmetproject.eu/assets/files/ELMET_Guide_EN.pdf>
- Priority: practical/background.
- Use: Practical guide for design, implementation, facilitation, and evaluation
  of educational escape rooms.
- Wizard relevance: Useful as a non-peer-reviewed but applied comparison for
  author-facing workflow and implementation checklists.
