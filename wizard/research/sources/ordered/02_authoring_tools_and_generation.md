# 02 - Authoring Tools, Model-Driven Design, and Generation

## Core Sources

### Laurent et al. 2022 - Serious-Game Authoring Tools

- Citation: Laurent, M., Monnier, S., Huguenin, A., Monaco, P.-B., & Jaccard,
  D. (2022). Design Principles for Serious Games Authoring Tools. International
  Journal of Serious Games, 9(4), 63-87.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.17083/ijsg.v9i4.458>
- Priority: core.
- Use: Design principles for powerful yet usable serious-game authoring tools.
- Wizard relevance: Directly supports the power/usability tradeoff for
  non-programmer authors and the need for abstraction layers.

### Ahmad et al. 2020 - Instructional Design in Authoring Tools

- Citation: Ahmad, A., Law, E. L.-C., & Moseley, A. (2020). Integrating
  Instructional Design Principles in Serious Games Authoring Tools: Insights
  from Systematic Literature Review. NordiCHI 2020, Article 46, 1-12.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.1145/3419249.3420133>
- Priority: core.
- Use: Systematic literature review on instructional-design support in serious
  game authoring tools.
- Wizard relevance: Supports making learning outcomes and instructional design
  explicit in the authoring workflow instead of treating them as afterthoughts.

### Roungas 2016 - Model-Driven Educational Game Design

- Citation: Roungas, B. (2016). A Model-driven Framework for Educational Game
  Design. International Journal of Serious Games, 3(3), 19-37.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.17083/ijsg.v3i3.126>
- Priority: core.
- Use: Model-driven environment that relates educational and entertainment game
  elements before implementation.
- Wizard relevance: Strong support for a config-first DEER model and a
  schema-driven authoring artifact.

### Shaker et al. 2016 - Procedural Content Generation in Games

- Citation: Shaker, N., Togelius, J., & Nelson, M. J. (2016). Procedural Content
  Generation in Games. Springer.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-319-42716-4>
- Priority: core.
- Use: Foundational PCG textbook.
- Wizard relevance: Supports seed/config-based generation, level generation,
  quest/story planning, generator evaluation, and reproducibility.

### Liapis et al. 2016 - Mixed-Initiative Content Creation

- Citation: Liapis, A., Smith, G., & Shaker, N. (2016). Mixed-initiative content
  creation. In Procedural Content Generation in Games, 195-214.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-319-42716-4_11>
- Priority: core.
- Use: Explains co-creative content-generation systems where humans and
  software both contribute.
- Wizard relevance: Justifies the generator as a collaborator that validates and
  fills technical structure while the educational author controls meaning.

### Hunicke et al. 2004 - MDA

- Citation: Hunicke, R., LeBlanc, M., & Zubek, R. (2004). MDA: A Formal Approach
  to Game Design and Game Research. Proceedings of the AAAI Workshop on
  Challenges in Game AI.
- URL: <https://users.cs.northwestern.edu/~hunicke/MDA.pdf>
- Priority: core/background.
- Use: Connects mechanics, dynamics, and aesthetics.
- Wizard relevance: Helps keep the authoring model linked to runtime behavior
  and intended player experience.

## Secondary and Background Sources

### Togelius et al. 2011 - Search-Based PCG

- Citation: Togelius, J., Yannakakis, G. N., Stanley, K. O., & Browne, C.
  (2011). Search-Based Procedural Content Generation: A Taxonomy and Survey.
  IEEE Transactions on Computational Intelligence and AI in Games, 3(3),
  172-186.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.1109/TCIAIG.2011.2148116>
- Priority: secondary.
- Use: Taxonomy and survey for generation as constrained search/evaluation.
- Wizard relevance: Useful later for layout fitness criteria, graph constraints,
  and generator evaluation.

### Lai et al. 2020 - Friendly Mixed-Initiative PCG

- Citation: Lai, G., Latham, W., & Leymarie, F. F. (2020). Towards Friendly
  Mixed Initiative Procedural Content Generation: Three Pillars of Industry.
  FDG 2020.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.1145/3402942.3402946>
- Priority: secondary.
- Use: Discusses designer-friendly mixed-initiative PCG.
- Wizard relevance: Supports human control, explainable suggestions, and
  placing the post-generator fine-tuning editor in future work.

### Moirai 2023 - No-Code Serious-Game Authoring

- Citation: Torres, A., Kapralos, B., Da Silva, C., Peisachovich, E., &
  Dubrowski, A. (2022). Moirai: A No-Code Virtual Serious Game Authoring
  Platform. Virtual Worlds, 1(2), 147-171.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.3390/virtualworlds1020009>
- Priority: background/comparison.
- Use: Example of no-code serious-game authoring for non-programmers.
- Wizard relevance: Useful comparison case for future related-work sections.
