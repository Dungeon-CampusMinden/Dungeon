# 03 - Evaluation and Methodology

Scope note: This file supports later evaluation and paper work. Evaluation,
telemetry, traceability, debriefing, and pre/post tests are not V0 Wizard
features.

## Core Sources

### Hevner et al. 2004 - Design Science

- Citation: Hevner, A. R., March, S. T., Park, J., & Ram, S. (2004). Design
  Science in Information Systems Research. MIS Quarterly, 28(1), 75-105.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.2307/25148625>
- Priority: core/background.
- Use: Foundational design-science framing.
- Wizard relevance: Frames the wizard as an artifact whose value is shown
  through building, demonstration, and evaluation.

### Peffers et al. 2007 - Design-Science Methodology

- Citation: Peffers, K., Tuunanen, T., Rothenberger, M. A., & Chatterjee, S.
  (2007). A Design Science Research Methodology for Information Systems
  Research. Journal of Management Information Systems, 24(3), 45-77.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.2753/MIS0742-1222240302>
- Priority: core/background.
- Use: Practical methodology for design-science research.
- Wizard relevance: Supports a WIP/full-paper structure: problem, objectives,
  design/development, demonstration, evaluation, communication.

### Lopez-Pernas 2024 - EER Meta-Analysis

- Citation: Lopez-Pernas, S. (2024). Educational Escape Rooms Are Effective
  Learning Activities Across Educational Levels and Contexts: A Meta-Analysis.
  IEEE Transactions on Learning Technologies, 17, 711-724.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.1109/TLT.2023.3328913>
- Priority: core.
- Use: Meta-analysis of educational escape-room learning effectiveness.
- Wizard relevance: Strong evidence base for educational escape rooms as a
  learning activity, while the wizard contribution remains authoring support.

### Veldkamp et al. 2022 - Learning During Gameplay

- Citation: Veldkamp, A., Niese, J. R., Heuvelmans, M., Knippels, M. C. P. J.,
  & van Joolingen, W. R. (2022). You escaped! How did you learn during
  gameplay? British Journal of Educational Technology, 53(5), 1430-1458.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.1111/bjet.13194>
- Priority: core/secondary.
- Use: Empirical study of learning, immersion, collaboration, and debriefing
  during educational escape-room gameplay.
- Wizard relevance: Supports evaluation dimensions beyond technical completion.

### Lopez-Pernas et al. 2023 - Learning Analytics

- Citation: Lopez-Pernas, S., Saqr, M., Gordillo, A., & Barra, E. (2023). A
  learning analytics perspective on educational escape rooms. Interactive
  Learning Environments, 31(10), 6509-6525.
- DOI: <https://doi.org/10.1080/10494820.2022.2041045>
- Priority: secondary/core for runtime evaluation.
- Use: Learning-analytics perspective on educational escape rooms.
- Wizard relevance: Supports runtime logging of progression, hint usage,
  sequence patterns, and completion data.
